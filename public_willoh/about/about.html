Design

  • I often sum up one of my most prominent passions to an interest and knack for graphic design. Often, this instills visions of digital art and social media adverstising in people's heads. While this is, perhaps, a fair assumption, I do not participate in social media, nor do I have any interest in doing so. Sure, I can illustrate both digitally and not to a respectable level, particularily for someone who never did any sort of formal study of anything artistic past middle school art class. This said, I am deeply interested in brand representation. I am super passionate about the idea of of fonts, colors, and images serving as an artistic representation of an idea, an identity. Of course, in this stage of life, I only pursue embarking in such graphic design as a hobby, and my highest achievement in regards to it is redesigning the logo for Penn State's International Student Council, included below.

    • This taken into consideration, it is most fair to say rather that I have a passion for practical design- design with intention, purpose, and function. What interests me in brand identity is the oppurtunity to inspire individuals to take action, or feel certain things, based entirely on intentional design. It isn't the drawing itself so much as the puzzle of navigating the idea of creating something memorable, but also representative of certian ideas/ideals.
