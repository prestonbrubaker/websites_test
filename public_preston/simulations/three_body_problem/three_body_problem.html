Preston's Simulation of the Three-Body Problem

Understanding the Three-Body Problem

The three-body problem demonstrates the complex gravitational interactions between three celestial objects. Here's what makes this simulation fascinating:

  • Gravitational Pull: As objects come closer, their gravitational attraction increases exponentially.
  • Sensitive Dependence: A minute change in position leads to significant variance over time, showing how chaotic celestial mechanics can be.
  • Unpredictable Orbits: The paths of bodies are highly sensitive, making long-term prediction a challenge.

In this simulation, we visualize how a tiny variation in initial conditions can lead to drastically different outcomes. The bottom of the canvas captures the trails left by planets, painting a picture of their dynamic interactions over time.
